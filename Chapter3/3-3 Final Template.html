3.3 The Lie Derivative

OK, so now we have a closed ideal – so what?  This brings us to the Lie derivative. Lie derivatives of vectors, functions, tensors and differential forms with respect to a vector are all defined.  Symbolically, the Lie derivative of a form with respect to a vector is represented as

\begin{equation}{\mathcal{L}_V}\left( \omega  \right)\notag\end{equation} It evaluates the change of a form $\omega $ along the flow defined by the vector field $V$.  One can take as a definition of this operation Cartan’s Magic Formula:

\begin{equation}{\mathcal{L}_V}\left( \omega  \right) = \left\langle {V,d\omega } \right\rangle  + d\left\langle {V,\omega } \right\rangle\end{equation} To make a glancing connection to Lie algebra, the Lie derivative of a vector with respect to another vector is just the Lie bracket of the two vectors.

\begin{equation}{\mathcal{L}_X}\left( Y \right) = \left[ {X,Y} \right]\;\notag\end{equation}

Note that the Lie derivative of a form is degree-preserving.  We will use this process to discover isovectors, vector fields that, when used in a Lie derivative of a form in a closed ideal, will deliver (at most) other members of the closed ideal (or linear combinations of them).  Specifically, if ${\omega ^\alpha }$ are generators of an ideal $I\left\{ {{\omega ^\alpha }} \right\}$, then isovectors of that ideal satisfy

\begin{equation}{\mathcal{L}_V}\left( {{\omega ^\alpha }} \right) = \Lambda _\beta ^\alpha  \wedge {\omega ^\beta }\end{equation}

for some $\Lambda _\beta ^\alpha $ of various degrees.  Suhubi’s Theorem 5.12.3 shows that such isovectors are also, automatically, isovectors of that ideal’s closure $I\left\{ {{\omega ^\alpha },d{\omega ^\beta }} \right\}$.  There is one more interesting factoid, whose utility remains to be seen:  when considering a closed ideal all of its characteristic vectors are also isovectors!  A similar condition is mentioned by Burke on page 261.  Isovectors will represent symmetries of the PDE encoded in the forms used to generate the ideal.  We need to jump to Suhubi’s Chapter 9 to explore this.
